Alphabet


The japanese language is comprised of 3 different alphabets Hirigana, Katakana, Kanji


Hiragana is the most use alphabet due to the fact that most japanese words are or can be in Hirigana


Katakana is used for non japanese words like foreign names or items


Romaji is the alphabet that shows you how to pronounce every japanese character for example: (る) romaji: RU


Kanji is technicaly chinese but used in japanese writing, Kanji is used for writing nouns, adjectives, adverbs and verbs.


All 3 of these alphabets are used alongside each other to create grammaticaly correct sentences.


Numbers


Most numbers in japanese are made up of single digits, some are different for example Hundered, thousand, million, and so on. Everything else is rather simple


Phonetics


This is how everything is pronounced as the japanese language is rather complex in how everything is pronounced.


A key example of this is the Hirigana character RU (る).

This is pronounced completely differently to how it looks. Pronouncing these types of characters correctly can take your japanese skills to a whole other level


Grammar


This is the understanding of how to structure sentences properly and having good Grammar will take your japanese to a whole other level.


If you have a good understanding of Grammar you will be able to hold a conversation with a native japanese person and sounds like a native at the same time as long as your phonetics are in check.
